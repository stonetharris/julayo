3-Stage Project in Akure, Nigeria

Akure, Nigeria has 400,000 people and only one public hospital. In Akure, people suffer from preventable and easily treatable diseases.

Stage 1: Primary Care

  • Primary care clinic and vaccination center.
  • Adult primary care, pediatrics, and gynecology.
  • Focus will be on preventative medicine (i.e. diabetes and cancer screenings) and health maintenance (i.e. blood pressure management) in addition to as well-child care.
  • Health education outreach program.
  • The staff will be fully Nigerian providing an opportunity for employment and community development.
  • This building will include solar power and a deep well to provide ongoing energy and water sources for phase 2 and 3.

Stage 2: School of medical sciences

  • Training programs for nursing, emergency medical technicians, pharmacy technicians, radiology technicians, medical assistants, medical secretaries and health educators.
  • Clinical clerkships will be held at the primary care clinic where appropriate.
  • The professors will come from both Nigeria and overseas.
  • Tuition will be at market rate with an opportunity for scholarships as funding allows.

Stage 3: Medical Center

75 bed hospital with full operating room suite and labor and delivery unit. Subspecialties included Orthopedics Infectious disease Ob/gyn Pediatrics General surgery There will be a bus that provides free transportation to the hospital from the surrounding communities.
